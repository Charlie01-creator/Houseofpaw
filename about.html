How We Began

Founded in 2015 by veterinarian Dr. Sarah Johnson and animal welfare advocate Michael Rodriguez, HOUSE OF PAWS started as a small foster home with just two kennels in a garage. Our first rescue was Hope, a severely malnourished stray found abandoned in an industrial park.

Hope's transformation - from our first rescue to thriving family pet

After Hope's remarkable recovery and adoption, Sarah and Michael realized the urgent need for specialized care for abused and abandoned dogs. They converted Michael's property into a proper shelter, and within a year, HOUSE OF PAWS became an official 501(c)(3) nonprofit.

Today, our state-of-the-art facility includes medical treatment rooms, recovery suites, behavioral training areas, and comfortable adoption meet-and-greet spaces. We've expanded our team to include veterinary specialists, behaviorists, and a network of foster homes throughout the region.

In 2023 alone, we rescued over 150 dogs, provided more than 300 low-cost spay/neuter surgeries to community pets, and educated over 2,000 children about responsible pet ownership through our school programs.

Our Core Beliefs

The principles that guide every decision we make

Compassion First

Every decision begins with empathy - for the animals in our care and the people we serve. We believe kindness is the foundation of all meaningful change.

Uncompromising Care

We never cut corners when it comes to health, safety, and quality of life. Each animal receives the same level of care we would want for our own pets.

Community Partnership

We believe in collaborating with local organizations, businesses, and individuals to create a network of support that extends beyond our walls.

Our Journey

Key milestones in our growth

2015

Founded with the rescue of Hope, our first success story. Established first foster network with 5 volunteer families.

2016

Became an official 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization. Rescued our 50th dog and hired first full-time staff member.

2018

Opened our current 5,000 sq ft facility with medical wing. Launched low-cost spay/neuter clinic for community pets.

2020

Launched community education programs reaching 5,000+ people. Celebrated our 200th successful adoption.

2023

Celebrated our 500th successful adoption. Expanded to include mobile veterinary unit for underserved areas.

2025

Opened second location in neighboring county. Launched foster training program certified by Animal Welfare Institute.
