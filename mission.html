Our Story

HOUSE OF PAWS was founded in 2015 with a simple goal: to rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome dogs in need while advocating for animal welfare in our community.

2015: The Beginning

Founded by Sarah Johnson after rescuing her first dog, Max, from an abusive situation. Started with just 5 foster homes and a dream.

2017: First Facility

Opened our first small rescue facility thanks to a generous donation. Capacity increased to house 15 dogs at a time.

2019: 501(c)(3) Status

Received official nonprofit status, allowing us to expand our programs and reach more dogs in need.

2021: Current Facility

Moved to our current 5-acre property with state-of-the-art kennels, play areas, and medical facilities.

Today

Over 1,000 dogs rescued and counting. 30+ active foster families. 15 staff members. 200+ volunteers.

Our Core Mission

At HOUSE OF PAWS, we're driven by a clear purpose that guides everything we do.

Rescue

We save dogs from high-risk shelters, abusive situations, and abandonment, providing immediate medical care and safety.

Rehabilitate

Each dog receives comprehensive veterinary care, behavioral assessment, and training to prepare them for adoption.

Rehome

We carefully match dogs with loving forever families through our rigorous adoption process and follow-up support.

Educate

Community education programs promote responsible pet ownership, spay/neuter initiatives, and animal welfare.

Advocate

We work with lawmakers to strengthen animal protection laws and improve conditions for all animals.

Support

Our programs help keep pets with their families through food banks, low-cost clinics, and crisis foster care.

Our Values

These principles guide every decision we make and every action we take.

Compassion First

Every dog is treated as an individual with unique needs and personality. We believe in kindness without exception.

Transparency

We maintain open books and clear communication about how donations are used and what outcomes we achieve.

Community

We're stronger together. Collaboration with other rescues, volunteers, and supporters amplifies our impact.

No-Kill Philosophy

We never euthanize for space or time. Dogs stay with us as long as it takes to find them the right home.

Responsibility

We take full responsibility for every dog we rescue, offering lifetime support to adopters when needed.

Innovation

We continuously improve our methods based on the latest research in animal behavior and welfare.
